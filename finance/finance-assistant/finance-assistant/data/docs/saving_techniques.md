# Saving Techniques to Build Wealth

## The Emergency Fund First

Before investing or aggressively saving for goals, build an emergency fund of 3 to 6 months of living expenses. This prevents you from going into debt when unexpected costs arise — medical bills, car repairs, job loss.

Keep this fund in a separate high-yield savings account so it earns interest but is not mixed with daily spending money.

## Automate Your Savings

The single most effective saving habit is automation. Set up automatic transfers from your salary account to a savings account on the same day you get paid. "Out of sight, out of mind" works powerfully for saving.

Even saving 5% automatically beats saving 20% manually for most people.

## The 24-Hour Rule for Purchases

Before any non-essential purchase above a certain threshold (e.g., Rs. 2,000), wait 24 hours. This simple delay eliminates a significant portion of impulse purchases. Many people find the urge disappears entirely after sleeping on it.

## Reduce Subscriptions

Audit all recurring subscriptions every 3 months. Streaming services, gym memberships, app subscriptions, and food delivery memberships add up to thousands per year without you noticing. Cancel anything you have not used in the past 30 days.

## Cook at Home More Often

Food is the most flexible spending category for most people. Eating out costs 3 to 5 times more than cooking at home per meal. Even reducing restaurant spending by 30% can free up substantial money monthly.

Meal prepping on weekends reduces weekday temptation to order food.

## Negotiate Bills

Most people never negotiate internet, insurance, or phone bills, but companies regularly offer discounts to customers who ask, especially if you mention competitor pricing. A single phone call can save thousands per year.

## The Savings Rate Goal

Track your savings rate — the percentage of income you save each month. Even 10% is a strong start. As income grows, keep lifestyle inflation in check and channel raises into savings rather than spending.

Financial independence research (the FIRE movement) shows that a 50% savings rate can allow someone to retire in roughly 17 years, regardless of income level.

## Saving on Utilities

- Switch off lights and appliances when leaving a room.
- Use energy-efficient bulbs and appliances.
- Compare electricity and gas providers annually.
- Reduce water heating costs with timer switches.

## Sinking Funds

A sinking fund is savings set aside for a known future expense — Eid gifts, car maintenance, annual insurance premiums, vacations. By saving a small amount monthly, you avoid a budget shock when the expense arrives.

Example: If car maintenance costs Rs. 24,000 a year, save Rs. 2,000 per month in a dedicated sinking fund.
